The Customer Pension Journey

Unpacking the Mindsets, Motivations, and Pains of Athora's Customers

A Tale of Two Mindsets

Our interviews revealed a deep split in how customers approach their finances. Pension planning isn't a one-size-fits-all journey; it's a landscape of diverse attitudes, behaviors, and emotional drivers. Understanding this duality is the first step toward smarter segmentation.

The Proactive Planner

📋

Meticulous, organized, and control-oriented. These customers use spreadsheets and apps to manage every detail, seeking stress reduction through financial order. They desire granular control and sophisticated tools.

The Present-Focused Intuitive

🏖️

Guided by a "live for today" philosophy. They find complex tools overwhelming and prefer simplified, automated solutions that require minimal active management. Their focus is on lifestyle, not intricate financial mechanics.

Unpacking the Pension Mindset

Dominant Customer Feelings Towards Pensions

The overwhelming sentiment is not positive. A significant emotional barrier of uncertainty, skepticism, and a feeling of powerlessness leads to widespread disengagement. Phrases like "far-from-my-bed-show" and "uncertain factor" were common.

Risk Appetite: A Spectrum of Caution

Customers' willingness to invest is heavily influenced by past experiences and perceived control. While some are eager to invest, many view it as "gambling" and prioritize the security of savings accounts, even with lower returns.

The Five Great Walls of Pension Planning

Customers face significant barriers on their journey. These five core pain points create a cycle of confusion, anxiety, and inaction that providers must break.

Complexity

🤯

"I snap nothing of the system." Customers are overwhelmed by jargon and complicated processes.

Uncertainty

👻

"I'm afraid my pension might not exist." Fear of loss, inflation, and mismanagement is rampant.

Passivity

🤷

"They don't take on an advisory role." Customers feel unsupported and desire proactive, personalized guidance.

Admin Burden

🗂️

"Transferring my old pot is too much hassle." Cumbersome processes lead to frustration and disorganization.

Distrust

😒

"Insurers have 'messed with' money." Historical issues have eroded trust, creating deep suspicion.

The Reactive Journey: Triggers for Action

Pension planning is rarely proactive. Engagement is sparked by "moments of truth"—significant life events that force a financial re-evaluation. This highlights critical opportunities for providers to offer timely, relevant support.

🏠

Major Life Changes

Buying a home, getting divorced, or changing jobs are the most powerful catalysts for financial engagement.

👶

Family Growth

The birth of a child immediately shifts priorities towards long-term family security and savings.

💔

Personal Crises

Health scares, accidents, or past financial trauma (like debt) create a powerful need for financial control and security.

🗣️

External Information

Conversations with friends, news about inflation, or even a podcast can spark curiosity and a desire to act.

What Customers Truly Want

Retirement Dreams: More Than Just Money

Retirement goals are deeply personal and lifestyle-oriented. Customers want to translate abstract pension figures into tangible life experiences, with maintaining their current lifestyle as the top priority.

The Ideal Support Model: A Hybrid Approach

Customers don't want to choose between digital and human. They want the best of both: digital tools for convenience and overview, and empathetic human advisors for complex, trust-based conversations.

The Path Forward for Athora

The opportunity is clear: shift from being a passive pension holder to a proactive financial partner. By embracing psychographic segmentation, simplifying communication, and offering a hybrid support model, Athora can break the cycle of disengagement, build trust, and empower customers to achieve the future they envision.
